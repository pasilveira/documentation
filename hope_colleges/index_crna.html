⚠️ Important: Nursing Schools Use the TEAS Exam, Not the ACT
Nursing program admissions are primarily driven by GPA, the ATI TEAS exam (Test of Essential Academic Skills — covers Reading, Math, Science, English), and prerequisite course grades. While a strong ACT demonstrates academic ability, the TEAS is what nursing schools actually require for admission. Hope's excellent GPA (4.56) is her strongest asset here.

🔬 CRNA vs PA: Choosing Your Path

🏥 CRNA – Nurse Anesthesia

  • Highest-paying APRN: avg $236k in NC
  • Highly specialized, focused practice
  • Autonomous practice in NC (no MD supervision)
  • Strong union/hospital system protections
  • BSN required — limits your undergrad options
  • 1–3 years CVICU/SICU/Trauma ICU required
  • DNP/DNAP required (doctoral level since 2025)
  • Longer path: 7–10 years total
  • High CRNA program rejection rates

💊 PA – Physician Assistant

  • Any undergrad major — more flexibility
  • Broad clinical scope (any specialty)
  • ~6–7 years total timeline
  • Avg NC salary: ~$125k
  • Lower salary ceiling vs CRNA
  • MD/DO supervision still common in many states
  • CASPA application, PA school very competitive
  • 1,000–3,000 patient care hours required

🔑 The BSN is Non-Negotiable — Unlike the PA path where any bachelor's degree works, CRNA programs require an active RN license with a BSN. This means only schools with accredited BSN programs are viable starting points. NC State (biology/pre-health), Wake Forest (undergrad only), and Salem College (no nursing) are effectively eliminated from the CRNA path.

ICU Type Matters — Not Just Any Nursing Experience

CRNA programs evaluate the quality of ICU experience, not just the hours. The most competitive applications come from:

💜 CVICU (Cardiovascular ICU) 💜 SICU (Surgical ICU) 💜 Trauma ICU (Level I center) ⚠️ MICU acceptable but less competitive ⚠️ Step-down / PACU not typically accepted ⚠️ Pediatric/Neonatal ICU: program-dependent

BSN Prerequisites to Know

Biology I & II Chemistry I & II Anatomy & Physiology I & II Microbiology Statistics Nutrition Developmental Psychology Medical Terminology ATI TEAS Exam (school-specific)

💡 Strategic Advice for Hope

1. ACT Score Matters Less for Nursing — GPA Is Queen
BSN programs primarily screen on GPA and TEAS scores. Hope's 4.56 GPA is her strongest asset for nursing admissions. Focus TEAS prep over ACT retake strategy if CRNA is the definitive goal.
2. Choose Your ICU City Strategically
The city where you do your ICU work heavily influences which CRNA programs you can realistically target. Raleigh → Duke or Wake Forest. Greenville → ECU. Greensboro → UNCG. Don't end up isolated in Wilmington if CRNA is the goal — plan the post-BSN move.
3. GPA in Your BSN Matters Too
CRNA programs are highly competitive and expect a BSN GPA of 3.5+. Many competitive programs want 3.7+. Choose a school where Hope can excel academically, not just survive.
4. Shadow a CRNA Now — Before Committing
This career path is long and demanding. Many students pivot after seeing what ICU nursing actually involves day-to-day. Try to shadow both a CRNA and an ICU nurse before committing to this path vs. the PA track.
5. Consider the PA vs. CRNA Trade-off Carefully
PA path: 6–7 years, any major, ~$125k salary, broad clinical variety.
CRNA path: 7–10 years, BSN required, ~$236k salary, highly specialized.
The CRNA salary advantage is roughly $111k/year — substantial over a 30-year career. But the path is longer, more restrictive, and the ICU years are intense.
